Why am I a part of Just Texas?

"Abortion is murder. Zygotes are people. Women are incubators." — This is the basic logic of politicians and activists who are dead set on overturning Roe V Wade and stripping away women’s heath care services along the way. Never mind that there is no scientific, moral, political, theological, or biblical consensus that personhood begins at conception. With about 60% of fertilized eggs never becoming implanted in the uterus, the human body as God created it cannot even live up to this extreme pro-birth standard.

The notion that women are incubators for life rather than whole lives in and of themselves is pure misogyny, thinly veiled. It is much better theology to believe that women have been endowed with the God-given reason and moral agency to be good stewards of their own reproductive lives, or at least the Rabbis thought so. Classic rabbinic teaching, of which I as a Christian am an inheritor, asserts that personhood begins at birth. Until delivery, a fetus is part of her mother’s body. A precious part, a potential life, but not a separate human.

I am pro-choice because I know from experience that it takes more than a single sexual act to create a person. It takes nine long, sometimes painful and complicated, months just to arrive at baby’s first breath. It goes on to require a lifetime of love and sacrifice to offer a child a life worth living. I am pro-choice because I trust that individual women know best whether they are prepared to mother for a lifetime. And what our children need is living examples of women who know their worth.
