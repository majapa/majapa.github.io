Responsible Ethnic Studies Textbook Coalition

We are forming a coalition of concerned Texans and organizations to oppose the approval of a deeply flawed Mexican-American Studies textbook at the State Board of Education in 2016. (See background on the textbook below.)

If you would like to be involved in this effort, please add your name. We’ll be in touch with more information soon.

 Background

The publisher of Mexican American Heritage is Cynthia Dunbar, a former member of the State Board of Education who hates public schools so much that she calls them a “tool of perversion” and “tyrannical.” She spent her time on the state board trying to force those same public schools to promote her divisive political agenda. Now she’s trying to do the same thing with this textbook.

Scholars across the country are already reviewing the textbook — the only Mexican-American studies text submitted for the state board’s approval this year. Many have already criticized the text’s"sanitized and whitewashed version of many important events affecting the Mexican-American population.” (Dr. Rogelio Sáenz, UT-San Antonio) Other scholars have condemned politicized distortions of history in the textbook, including claims that the foundations of U.S. government are found in the Bible.
