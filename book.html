Moses & America:
What didn't he do?

In school, we all learned about America's founders like George Washington, Thomas Jefferson, and Ben Franklin — but what about the most influential of them all? According to the Texas State Board of Education, students should also be learning about Moses.

You read that right — and Moses could soon be featured in American government textboks across Texas and the nation.

One government textbook proposed in Texas this year tells students that the roots of democratic government in America date back “thousands of years to Old Testament texts and Biblical figures such as Moses and Solomon.” Scholars say this claim is nonsense. But right-wing politicians on the State Board of Education have insisted that the new textbooks include this wildly distorted version of history.

History has proven that the Texas SBOE is unlikely to change its tune, regardless of how historically inaccurate their claims are. But textbook publishers have an obligation to present history that is free of partisan bias or religious ideology. And that's what we're asking them to do by providing revised textbooks to the SBOE before its final vote on Nov. 17.

Tell the publishers: Remove the exaggerated influences of Moses in US government and history textbooks.
